Workshop Scope

The Semantic Web/Cloud Intelligent Systems Management workshop brings together researchers and practitioners to exchange ideas and results on intelligent methods applied to Web- and Cloud-based systems. All accepted papers will be published in the Springer Lecture Notes in Data Engineering and Communication Technologies series and indexed by EI and SCOPUS. The workshop particularly encourages contributions addressing the convergence of Semantic Web technologies, Cloud–Edge computing, and Artificial Intelligence, with a strong focus on real-world systems and applications.

Topics of Interest

  • Semantic Web and Knowledge Graphs for Cloud–Edge–AI Systems
  • Cloud–Edge–IoT Continuum Architectures and Programming Models
  • AI-Driven Cloud and Service Management
  • Large Language Models and Generative AI for Service Discovery, Composition, and Management
  • Semantic Interoperability and Data Spaces
  • Intelligent Service Discovery, Brokering, and Orchestration
  • Autonomous Agents and Multi-Agent Systems for Distributed Environments
  • Knowledge Management and Reasoning in Cloud and Edge Systems
  • Process Mining and Intelligent Business Process Management
  • Service-Level Agreements, QoS, and Trust Management
  • Explainable, Trustworthy, and Responsible AI for Cloud Services
  • Natural Language Processing for Cloud and Web-Based Systems
  • Security, Privacy, and Compliance in Semantic and AI-Enabled Clouds
  • Sustainable and Energy-Aware Cloud and Edge Computing
  • Real-World Applications and Industrial Use Cases
