The Semantic Web/Cloud Intelligent Systems Management workshop brings together scientists, engineers, computer users and students to exchange ideas, experiences and research results on intelligent methods applied to Web and Cloud-based systems. All accepted papers will be included in the conference proceedings of the Lecture Notes in Data Engineering and Communication Technologies series published by Springer, indexed by EI and SCOPUS.

Topics

  • Information Retrieval
  • Semantic Grid, Cloud and Web
  • Cloud Infrastructures and Programming Paradigms
  • Service Level Agreements
  • Cloud Resource Negotiation and Brokering
  • Ontologies
  • Intelligent Agents
  • Knowledge Management
  • Semantic Web and Semantic Web Services
  • Business Processes
  • Machine Learning
  • Natural Language Processing and Deep Learning
  • Generative Artificial Intelligence
  • Process Mining
  • Multi-Agent Systems

Paper Submission Guidelines

Workshop papers should be a maximum of 10 pages long. Authors may add up to two extra pages with the appropriate fee. Papers must follow the Springer Lecture Notes template and be submitted in PDF format.

Publication

All accepted papers will appear in the Springer Lecture Notes in Networks and Systems series and will be indexed in major scientific databases. Selected papers may be invited to submit extended versions to international journal special issues.
