Call for Papers

Digital methods for humanities research and cultural knowledge

The International Workshop on Digital Humanities and Digital World (DHDW 2027) provides a forum for work at the intersection of Digital Humanities, Artificial Intelligence, Data Science and Information Technologies.

We welcome original contributions on computational methods, tools and applications for the analysis, preservation, interpretation and dissemination of cultural, historical and social data. Contributions may present new methods, resources, case studies, systems or critical evaluations.

The workshop is open to computer scientists, digital humanists, librarians, archivists, museum professionals and researchers working on interdisciplinary digital projects.

Topics

Areas of interest

Topics include, but are not limited to:

Digital Humanities and Cultural Heritage

  • Digital libraries, archives and scholarly editions
  • Digital history and computational humanities
  • Digitisation, preservation and access
  • Museums, archaeology and cultural heritage applications
  • Digital storytelling and public humanities

Artificial Intelligence and Data Analysis

  • Natural language processing and text mining
  • Large language models for humanities research
  • Multimodal analysis of text, images and audio
  • Information extraction and classification
  • Responsible and explainable AI

Semantic Technologies

  • Ontologies and knowledge graphs
  • Semantic annotation and metadata
  • Linked Open Data and FAIR data
  • Knowledge representation and reasoning
  • Interoperability of cultural data

Digital Society and Education

  • Misinformation, digital media and online discourse
  • Computational social science
  • Digital memory, identity and participation
  • Digital learning and research infrastructures
  • Ethical, legal and societal issues
Submission

Paper preparation and submission

Submission requirements

  • Submit an original paper in PDF format.
  • Workshop papers may be up to 10 pages, including figures and references.
  • Up to two additional pages are allowed subject to the AINA extra-page fee.
  • Use the Springer Lecture Notes proceedings style.
  • At least one author of each accepted paper must register, attend and present the paper.
Submit / author guidelines ↗

Publication

Accepted and presented papers will be included in the AINA 2027 workshop proceedings, subject to the conference registration and publication requirements.

The proceedings will be published by Springer in the Lecture Notes in Data Engineering and Communication Technologies (LNDECT) series.

Submission track: use the DHDW 2027 workshop track when it becomes available in the AINA submission system. Until then, the button opens the official AINA author page.
